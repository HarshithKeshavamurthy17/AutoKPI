**What they do**: Identify outliers and unusual patterns in your data

**Types**:

### 4. üìä Distribution Analysis KPIs
**What they do**: Analyze data distributions and concentration patterns

**Types**:

**Insights Provided**:

- Variability metrics
- Segmentation opportunities

**Example KPIs**:
- "Amount Concentration (80/20 Rule)" - Shows if 20% of records drive 80% of value

- "Spending Score Variability" - Measures how much values vary

**Visualizations**:

## üåü Creative KPI Examples

### Example 1: Weekly Pattern Detection
**KPI**: "Weekly Pattern in Amount"
**Insight**: "Monday is your best performing day (avg: $450), while Sunday is lowest ($320). This pattern suggests day-of-week effects."

### Example 2: Performance Gap Analysis
**KPI**: "Revenue Performance Gap"
**Insight**: "The gap between best (Electronics: $500) and worst (Books: $120) performers is 316%. This represents a significant opportunity."

**Insight**: "8% of Amount values are statistical anomalies (3+ standard deviations). Review for data entry errors."
**Action**: "Review outlier values for data entry errors. Consider outlier treatment (removal, winsorization, or separate analysis)."

**KPI**: "Amount Trend Change"
**Insight**: "Amount increased by 15% from the first half ($350) to the second half ($405) of the period."
**Action**: "Continue the positive momentum. Consider forecasting or investigating factors driving the increasing trend."
